What is Lung Cancer?


According to the National Cancer Registry, approximately two lakh (200,000) people are diagnosed with lung cancer every year in India. Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the cells of the lungs, typically in the cells that line the air passages. It is the leading cause of cancer deaths worldwide, as it often goes undetected until it has spread to other parts of the body. The most common type of lung cancer is non-small cell lung cancer, which is further divided into different subtypes based on the specific cell type and stage of progression.

Finding lung cancer early saves lives. It's a fact.
